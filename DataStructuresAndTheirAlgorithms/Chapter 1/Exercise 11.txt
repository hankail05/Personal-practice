Q.

Which of the following are true for any real numbers x and y?
Explain or give a counterexample.

A.

a. floor(x+y) >= floor(x) + floor(y)

If a sum of fractional parts of x and y is greater than or equal to 1,
floor(x+y) = floor(x) + floor(y) + 1, true.
If a sum of fractional parts of x and y is lesser than 1,
floor(x+y) = floor(x) + floor(y), true.

b. floor(ceiling(x)) + 1 = ceiling(floor(x))

ceiling(x) = floor(x)+1. The outer flooring and ceiling don't change x. false

c. floor(round(x)) = round(floor(x))

4.9 -> floor(round(4.9)) = 5, round(floor(x)) = 4. false
