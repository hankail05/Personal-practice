Q.

Which of the following are true for any real numbers $x$ and $y$?
Explain or give a counterexample.

a. $\lfloor x+y \rfloor \ge \lfloor x \rfloor + \lfloor y \rfloor$

b. $\lfloor \lceil x \rceil \rfloor + 1 = \lceil \lfloor x \rfloor \rceil$

c. $\lfloor |x| \rfloor = | \lfloor x \rfloor |$

A.

a.

If a sum of fractional parts of x and y is greater than or equal to 1,
$\lfloor x+y \rfloor = \lfloor x \rfloor + \lfloor y \rfloor + 1$, true.
If a sum of fractional parts of x and y is lesser than 1,
$\lfloor x+y \rfloor = \lfloor x \rfloor + \lfloor y \rfloor$, true.

b.

$\lceil x \rceil = \lfloor x \rfloor + 1$. The outer floor and ceiling don't change the results. false

c.

$4.9 \rightarrow \lfloor |4.9| \rfloor = 5, |\lfloor x \rfloor| = 4$. false
